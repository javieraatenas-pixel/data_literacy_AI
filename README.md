# Data, AI and Citizenship Literacy  
## Open Educational Resources (OER) Collection

Welcome to this repository of Open Educational Resources (OER) focused on **data literacy, artificial intelligence (AI) literacy, and citizenship literacy**.

This space curates, organises, and shares openly licensed materials designed to support educators, learners, and practitioners in developing **critical, ethical, and practical understanding of data and AI in society**.

---

## 🌍 About this repository

In today’s data-driven and AI-mediated world, it is essential for everyone—not only specialists—to develop the skills to **understand, interpret, question, and use data and AI responsibly**.

This repository provides a central hub of openly accessible resources that support:

- **Data literacy** — the ability to read, analyse, interpret, and communicate data  
- **AI literacy** — the ability to understand, evaluate, and critically engage with AI systems  
- **Citizenship literacy** — the capacity to participate ethically and responsibly in a digital and datafied society  

Together, these literacies help foster informed, active, and responsible citizens capable of engaging in democratic, social, and technological contexts.

---

## 📚 What you will find

This collection brings together open and reusable educational materials, including:

- Teaching resources and learning activities  
- Open courses and curricula  
- Frameworks, toolkits, and guides  
- Case studies and examples of practice  
- Research-informed pedagogical approaches  

All materials are selected to support **open education practices** and **inclusive learning design**.

---

## 🎯 Key focus

### 1. Openness  
Resources are openly licensed where possible, supporting reuse, adaptation, and sharing.

### 2. Critical and ethical engagement  
Materials encourage reflection on:

- Bias, fairness, and data justice  
- Ethical implications of AI  
- Power, participation, and representation  

### 3. Interdisciplinary learning  
Data and AI literacy are treated as transversal competences that cut across disciplines and contexts.

### 4. Citizenship and participation  
The resources aim to empower learners to become:

- Critical users of data and AI  
- Active contributors to knowledge and society  
- Responsible digital citizens
